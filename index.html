Research

I work on post-training methods that make large language models reason and act more reliably.

My focus is reinforcement learning for LLMs and on-policy distillation — training models along their own sampled trajectories rather than fixed targets. A recurring thread in my work is credit assignment in reasoning models: how to attribute a final outcome back to the individual tokens and steps that produced it, drawing on ideas like eligibility traces and temporal-difference learning adapted to the language-model setting.

I also build the training infrastructure this research depends on, and work on LLM agents and personalization — internalizing user preferences and tool-use behavior into model weights.
